       This visualization employs what The Financial Times vocabulary defines as "ordered proportional symbols." This technique is appropriate when there are significant variations in values or when differences between specific values are not crucial. While this visualization adheres to two of the principles outlined by Kennedy et al.         For instance, the term "last five years" is an indexical phrase, which can lead to ambiguity. The visualization's layout is rather messy, lacking a logical spatial organization of the bubbles. The four bubbles representing high values (UK, Canada, Australia, and the U.S.) are arranged one within the other, while medium values are positioned vertically toward the right edge of the visualization. Meanwhile, the smallest values (China and Italy) are represented as small bubbles at the bottom left. This illogical organization increases cognitive load, making it challenging to comprehend why the USA and Canada, and the UK and Australia, are situated within one another.

The lack of context suggests that this visualization targets a specialist audience, which can lead to higher cognitive loadsâ€”both germane, stemming from the general public's unfamiliarity with the information and its importance, and extraneous, resulting from the unorganized design choices mentioned above.
